The Eduba frame

We call it the 60 / 30 / 10 read.

Most enterprise workflows break down cleanly when you ask which layer each part belongs on. Skip the distinction and teams burn Gemini tokens on problems Postgres would solve in 40 milliseconds, and miss the real agentic opportunity hiding under the compliance and handoff work.

60%
Traditional code and data
The work that belongs on Postgres, BigQuery, or plain application logic. Fast, cheap, guaranteed.
30%
Rule-based logic
Decision trees, routing, policy enforcement. Deterministic, auditable, boring in the useful way.
10%
Genuine agent territory
The work where Gemini, ADK, and Agentspace actually earn their keep. Judgment, synthesis, multi-step reasoning.

Applied to a Vertex AI and ADK customer, the engagement produces three deliverables.

01
Layer map of the target workflowEach step tagged with the layer it should live on, with the reasoning written down.
02
Governance framework keyed to ADKA context architecture the internal audit team can read, keyed to the ADK agent structure.
03
Prioritized build listWhich agents to ship first, which to defer, and which not to build at all.
